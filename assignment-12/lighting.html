More lights, smaller aperture, less lights, bigger aperture

Lights go through the aperture which control the amount of the light. Look at the image. f1.4 has larger circle while f32 has smaller circle. Aperture is same as pupil. When you are in dark, you can't see anything; however, after a few minute, you can see something. Your pupil gets bigger slowly in the dark to get the lights go through your eye so you can see subjects in the dark.

It works opposite way when you are in lighter space. When you are in the classroom that lights turned off and suddenly when the lights turned on, you can't open your eyes well because it is too bright. It is because of your pupil. Your puil became bigger, and when the lights turned on, a lot of lights went through your eye. Then, your pupil slowly gets smaller to reduce the amount of lights to your eye.

It is exactly same as the aperture. Bigger aperture (f1.4), more lights go through, smllaer paerture (f32), less lights go through.

How the shutter speed works?

Think about what I just explained about your pupil. It takes some time to see something in dark space. Because it is too dark, you need some time to adapt in dark light slowly and slowly. Right? This concept of "slow" and "time" is the concpet of shutter speed in the camera.
